# Picopia NPCs, Creatures, and Homes

## Creature Role

Creatures are the social layer of the restoration loop.

- Creatures give short quests before opening abilities or recipes.
- Some creatures award badges for important quest milestones.
- Creatures can be fed with simple inventory items.
- Grass homes or nests are built to invite creatures into the clearing.
- Creatures can be assigned to or settled in suitable homes.
- Home hints explain what kind of home each creature needs.
- Creature furniture improves comfort and raises living conditions.
- A creature living nearby can enable contextual actions, such as lighting a campfire or building the player home.
- Discovering new creatures and improving their living conditions raises the environment level.
- Creature names and designs must be original fantasy-console creatures, not direct external character references.

## Interaction Model

Creature interaction should stay lightweight:

- One clear request.
- One clear completion message.
- Optional feeding requirement for trust or help.
- Optional hint about preferred home or furniture.
- Optional contextual helper action after the creature trusts the player or lives nearby.

## Ability Unlocks Through Creatures

Abilities should be framed as help from local creatures rather than abstract upgrades.

A creature can:

- Introduce a quest.
- Ask for food, restored habitat, or a crafted object.
- Unlock the related ability when the quest is complete.
- Give a badge for an important milestone.

Example unlock direction:

- A watering creature can unlock Watbit after Picoblob revives dry plants.
- A rototiller-style creature can improve Tillbit or prepare farm plots.
- A builder creature can help construct the player home after the blueprint is bought.
- A fire-capable creature can light a crafted campfire.
- A moisture-aware creature can comment on town humidity and point toward dry restoration targets.

## Homes

Grass homes or nests should be early structures that attract or house local creatures.

Home rules:

- A crafted grass home uses grass clippings.
- Homes invite creatures into the restored area.
- Some creatures require a suitable home before moving in.
- Home hints explain what kind of home a creature wants.
- Furniture improves home comfort and living conditions.
- Improved living conditions raise the environment level.

## Feeding

Feeding creatures can support:

- Trust progression.
- Ability unlocks.
- Recipe unlocks.
- Creature attraction to homes.
- Energy and food economy teaching.

Food should come from trees, plants, vegetables, berries, fruit, or snacks.

## Creature Comfort

Creature furniture should make the restored land feel lived-in.

Furniture can:

- Improve creature comfort.
- Raise living-condition level.
- Raise environment level.
- Unlock hints, contextual help, or small rewards.

## Originality Requirement

All creatures should be original fantasy-console designs. They can evoke broad nature, garden, moisture, fire, or building roles, but should not directly copy external character names, sprites, or copyrighted designs.
