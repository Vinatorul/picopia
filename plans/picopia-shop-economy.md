# Picopia Shop, Economy, and Environment Level

## Resources and Currency

Pixels are the current prototype currency and can later map to money.

Current resource rules:

- Chopbit cutting bush: +1 px.
- Smashbit breaking rock: +1 px.
- Sproutbit growing flower: +1 px in the current prototype.
- Challenge completion: money reward for shop purchases.
- Sproutbit growing grass or bush: no pixels.
- Watbit hydration: no pixels.
- Tillbit soil preparation: no pixels.

Current and planned costs:

- Grass costs 0 px.
- Bush costs 0 px.
- Flower planting should eventually require seeds bought from the shop.
- Tiny home costs 30 px in the current prototype.
- Player home requires a shop-bought blueprint before construction.

## Shop Role

The shop should convert restoration progress into new options without replacing exploration and creature quests.

The shop can sell:

- Items.
- Food or recovery supplies.
- Vegetable seeds.
- Flower seeds.
- Recipes.
- Blueprints.
- Player-home blueprint.

## Environment Level

The environment level measures how alive and comfortable the restored area is.

It should increase when the player:

- Discovers new creatures.
- Builds suitable homes.
- Crafts creature furniture.
- Feeds creatures.
- Improves creature living conditions.
- Restores meaningful terrain or town features.

## Environment-Level Gating

The environment level controls available challenges and shop stock.

Progression examples:

- Low level: basic items and early grass-home support.
- Mid level: flower seeds, vegetable seeds, simple recipes, creature furniture recipes.
- Higher level: blueprint stock, harder challenges, specialized creature support items.

Shop stock should visibly expand as restoration progresses.

## Challenge Rewards

Challenges should reward:

- Money for shop purchases.
- Items for crafting, feeding, or farming.
- Badges for milestone achievements.

Early challenge examples:

- Find specific creatures in the clearing.
- Water dry trees.
- Gather berries.
- Gather branches or twigs.

## Player Home Economy

The player should eventually build their own home.

Required flow:

1. Raise environment level enough to unlock the blueprint in the shop.
2. Buy the player-home blueprint.
3. Meet or recruit a builder creature.
4. Build the player home with creature help.
